Soldering Toggle Switches

Making sure the toggle switches are well plated against the circuit board.

  1. Put the toggle switch in place.
  2. Solder the 'middle leg' while pressing the switch against the circuit board.
  3. Verify that the switch is weel in place : plated against the circuit board and verticaly align.
  4. Do not solder the other legs. You will do this once the front panel is in place.

Soldering Rotary Switches

Making sure the rotary switches are well plated against the circuit board.

  1. Put the rotary switch in place.
  2. Solder one of the 'ouside leg' while pressing the switch against the circuit board.
  3. Solder the oppposite leg.
  4. While pressing the switch against the circuit board, use your soldering iron to heat the leg you soldered in step 2. (If the switch was not perfectly in place , you might hear a 'tack').
  5. Do the same for the opposite leg you just soldered in step 3.
  6. The switch is now perfectly plated against the circuit board. Solder the other legs.

Soldering Potentiometers

Making sure the potentiometers are well aligned.

  1. At this point, you should have the rotary and toggle switches in place on the circuit board.
  2. Put potentiometers in place, remove any nuts or washers.
  3. For each potentiometer put the 'x mm' spacer in place.
  4. Put the front panel in place. This might be a bit tricky : Use a plastic tool to reach and align potentiometeres that are not aligned.
    Make sure that the panel is well plated against the rotary and toggle switches.
  5. Secure the potentiometer by putting theyr washer and nuts.
  6. Verify that the 'Control Board' circuit board is parallel to the front panel
  7. Solder the potentiometers. It's a good pratice to only solder the middle leg, check the overall alignment of the board (parallel to the front panel) then solder any other legs.

Help

  • Next Step :

    Use the pagination buttons. You can also use the SPACE BAR key - Leave it pressed to quickly scroll thru steps.

  • Previous Step :

    Use the pagination buttons. You can also use the B key - Leave it pressed to quickly scroll thru steps.

  • Zoom In :

    Click or Tap any region within the circuit board to zoom in.

  • Zoom Out :

    Click or Tap any region within the circuit board to zoom out. You can also use the ESC key.

  • Zoom special :

    Use the P key to iterate thru components while in zoom mode.

  • Mark a component as soldered : Clicking on a component reference mark it as soldered, blinking will be removed and color will be updated.
